Ingredients

  • Tomatoes - You can use fresh, canned, or tomato products like crushed tomatoes or tomato paste
  • Olive Oil - Often used for sautéing onions and garlic before adding tomatoes
  • Onion - Adds flavor and depth to the sauce
  • Garlic - Adds a rich, aromatic flavor.
  • Salt and Pepper - For seasoning to taste
  • Sugar (optional) - Sometimes used to balance the acidity of the tomatoes
  • Herbs - Common herbs include basil, oregano, thyme, and bay leaves for added flavor
  • Red Pepper Flakes (optional) - Adds a bit of heat if desired
  • Balsamic Vinegar (optional) - Adds a hint of sweetness and acidity
  • Fresh Herbs (optional) - Such as basil, parsley, or cilantro for garnish

    • Steps!

    1. Prepare the Meatballs
      In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, milk, chopped onion, minced garlic, chopped parsley, and the egg. Season the mixture with salt and pepper. Mix everything together until well combined. Form the mixture into meatballs, about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter, and place them on a plate.
    2. Prepare the Tomato Sauce
      In a large saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add finely chopped onion and minced garlic and sauté until they become translucent. Add the tomatoes (either fresh, canned, crushed, or tomato paste) to the saucepan. Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and herbs of your choice (such as basil and oregano). If you prefer a sweeter sauce, add a pinch of sugar to balance the acidity. Let the sauce simmer on low heat for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    3. Cook the Meatballs
      Carefully place the meatballs into the simmering tomato sauce. Gently stir to coat the meatballs with the sauce. Cover the saucepan and let the meatballs simmer in the sauce for approximately 20-30 minutes or until they are cooked through.
    4. Serve
      Serve your delicious meatballs hot, either over cooked spaghetti, in a sub roll for sandwiches, or as an appetizer. Spoon some of the flavorful tomato sauce over the meatballs. Garnish with fresh herbs like basil or parsley for a burst of color and flavor.
